54 /**
55  * \brief Model the error rate for different modulations.
56  *
57  * A packet of interest (e.g., a packet can potentially be received by the MAC)
58  * is divided into chunks. Each chunk is related to an start/end receiving event.
59  * For each chunk, it calculates the ratio (SINR) between received power of packet
60  * of interest and summation of noise and interfering power of all the other incoming
61  * packets. Then, it will calculate the success rate of the chunk based on
62  * BER of the modulation. The success reception rate of the packet is derived from
63  * the success rate of all chunks.
64  *
65  * The 802.11b modulations:
66  * - 1 Mbps mode is based on DBPSK. BER is from equation 5.2-69 from John G. Proakis
67  * Digitial Communications, 2001 edition
68  * - 2 Mbps model is based on DQPSK. Equation 8 from "Tight bounds and accurate
69  * approximations for dqpsk transmission bit error rate", G. Ferrari and G.E. Corazza
70  * ELECTRONICS LETTERS, 40(20):1284-1285, September 2004
71  * - 5.5 Mbps and 11 Mbps are based on equations (18) and (17) from "Properties and
72  * performance of the ieee 802.11b complementarycode-key signal sets",
73  * Michael B. Pursley and Thomas C. Royster. IEEE TRANSACTIONS ON COMMUNICATIONS,
74  * 57(2):440-449, February 2009.
75  * - More detailed description and validation can be found in
76  * <a href="http://www.nsnam.org/~pei/80211b.pdf">http://www.nsnam.org/~pei/80211b.pdf</a>
77  */
78 double WiFi_80211b_DsssDqpskCck11_SuccessRate(double sinr,uint32_t nbits)
79 {
80  double ber;
